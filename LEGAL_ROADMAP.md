# IsoniaOS Legal Roadmap

**Version:** Draft v0.2
**Status:** Draft
**Project:** IsoniaOS
**Language:** English

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the legal-phasing roadmap for IsoniaOS and ISO.

It is not legal advice. It is a planning document for legal review, governance structuring, disclosures, entity setup, and future economic module feasibility.

No revenue-sharing rights exist at launch. Any future economic participation module will require a separate governance proposal, independent legal review, updated disclosures, and, where required, a regulated structure.

---

## 2. Phase 0 - Documentation and Legal Risk Minimization

Goals:

- no revenue-sharing rights at launch;
- no yield promises;
- no equity language;
- no guaranteed return language;
- prepare legal questions list;
- prepare token classification memo template;
- prepare counsel onboarding package.

Documentation requirements:

- ISO is not described as equity.
- ISO is not described as shares, dividends, profit rights, or guaranteed yield.
- Curve Reserve is not described as treasury or operational runway.
- USDC issuer/freeze/depeg risks are disclosed.
- ETH is not described as launch reserve or redemption backing.
- Future economic participation is conditional and legally reviewed.

---

## 3. Phase 1 - Initial Income and Legal Fund Activation

Goals:

- allocate part of fees to Legal & Compliance Fund;
- governance/Council approves first legal budget;
- engage specialists for initial review.

Legal review topics:

- token classification;
- bonding curve communications;
- identity/KYC/KYB obligations;
- treasury disclosure;
- Curve Reserve invariant;
- USDC impairment emergency clause;
- jurisdictional restrictions;
- future entity setup.

---

## 4. Phase 2 - Entity Setup

Goals:

- form association/foundation-like entity when funded;
- form commercial Lda when funded;
- define IP ownership and licensing between entities;
- define reporting obligations.

Target mature structure:

1. A Portuguese association / foundation-like entity for domains, GitHub organization, open-source IP, trademarks, governance constitution, public treasury coordination, grants, and Protocol Council process.
2. A separate commercial company, likely an Lda, for SaaS, enterprise deployments, custom integrations, support contracts, consulting, private deployments, SLA, payroll, and operational invoices.

Do not state that these entities already exist unless future entity records confirm that they do.

---

## 5. Phase 3 - Token Launch Legal Review

Goals:

- review bonding curve docs;
- review public communications;
- review user terms;
- review KYC/KYB/privacy flow;
- review jurisdictional restrictions;
- review treasury and reserve disclosures.

The launch Curve Reserve remains native USDC-only unless governance, legal review, security review, and updated disclosures approve a different design before launch.

ETH is not the launch redemption asset and is not the launch unit of account for the ISO bonding curve.

---

## 6. Phase 4 - Value-Accrual Feasibility

Potential mechanisms to review:

- buyback/burn;
- buyback-to-treasury;
- fee rebates;
- staking incentives;
- other value-accrual mechanisms.

Requirements:

- legal review;
- independent legal opinion where appropriate;
- updated disclosures;
- governance approval.

No mechanism should be described as active until it has passed the required legal, governance, and disclosure process.

---

## 7. Phase 5 - Regulated Economic Participation, Only If Legally Permitted

Requirements:

- separate legal structure if required;
- eligible participant rules;
- KYC/KYB;
- tax/reporting analysis;
- transfer restrictions if needed;
- no activation without governance and legal approval.

This phase is optional and conditional. It must not be marketed as guaranteed, inevitable, or present at launch.

---

## 8. Two-Entity Structure Roadmap

The mature target structure separates public-governance stewardship from commercial operations.

Association / foundation-like entity responsibilities:

- domains;
- GitHub organization;
- open-source IP;
- trademarks;
- governance constitution;
- public treasury coordination;
- grants;
- Protocol Council process.

Commercial company, likely Lda, responsibilities:

- SaaS;
- enterprise deployments;
- custom integrations;
- support contracts;
- consulting;
- private deployments;
- SLA;
- payroll and operational invoices.

This is a roadmap item to be executed as funds become available.

---

## 9. Commercial Transparency and Confidentiality

IsoniaOS should balance public accountability with client confidentiality.

Commercial clients may not want their identity, exact contract terms, or exact payment amounts disclosed.

The commercial entity should publish periodic aggregated reports. Reports should avoid deanonymizing clients.

Quarterly report draft fields:

- total SaaS revenue;
- total enterprise/custom revenue;
- total support revenue;
- total infrastructure costs;
- total payroll/admin costs;
- contribution to association/treasury;
- runway estimate;
- risk notes;
- client concentration ranges without naming clients.

Individual client names, exact invoices, private terms, SLA details, and custom technical details should not be disclosed unless explicitly permitted.

---

## 10. Phased Transfer of Non-Profit Governance

Phase 1:

- Founder/team controls the association/foundation-like entity.
- Protocol Council is advisory.

Phase 2:

- Council receives veto on IP, domain, GitHub, trademark transfer, and major treasury movement.

Phase 3:

- Council approves major protocol grants, legal fund executors, and public roadmap changes.

Phase 4:

- Council appoints/removes part of the association/foundation board.

Phase 5:

- Association/foundation bylaws formally recognize Protocol Council as a core governance body.

---

## 11. Legal Questions List

Initial questions for counsel:

- How should ISO be classified in relevant jurisdictions?
- What disclosures are required for a USDC-denominated bonding curve?
- What identity/KYC/KYB requirements apply to primary purchases or governance activation?
- What restrictions apply to future value-accrual mechanisms?
- What entity structure best separates open-source governance stewardship and commercial operations?
- How should Curve Reserve and treasury funds be disclosed?
- How should USDC issuer/freeze/depeg risks be disclosed?
- What terms are needed for grants, donations, SaaS contributions, and treasury reporting?
- What jurisdictional restrictions are needed before launch?
- What governance process is legally appropriate for future economic participation modules?
