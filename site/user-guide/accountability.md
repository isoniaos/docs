# Accountability

Accountability is how IsoniaOS tracks follow-through after a decision.

## What To Track

For each approved decision that needs follow-up, track:

- responsible person or group;
- due date or expected window;
- current status;
- evidence;
- milestone notes;
- blocker;
- failure or cancellation reason;
- completion review;
- update history.

## Basic Flow

1. Open the accountability record.
2. Check the owner and due date.
3. Review the current status.
4. Attach or inspect evidence.
5. Add a note if the work is blocked, late, failed, cancelled, or complete.
6. Ask a reviewer to confirm completion when needed.
7. Keep the final record in governance memory.

## Status Guide

| Status | What users should do |
| --- | --- |
| Waiting | Confirm who owns the next step. |
| In progress | Check due date and expected evidence. |
| Blocked | Record the blocker and next review point. |
| Executed | Check whether final review is still needed. |
| Complete | Confirm evidence and preserve the record. |
| Failed | Record what failed and why. |
| Cancelled | Record who cancelled follow-up and why. |
| Unknown | Do not treat the record as complete. Find the missing source or update status. |

## Example

Community Grants DAO assigns a contributor to deliver a report by the end of the month. The record starts as waiting, moves to in progress, receives a deliverable link, and then becomes complete after reviewer confirmation.
