# Why Governance Lifecycle

Governance is not a vote. Governance is a lifecycle.

Voting is important, but a mature governance system also needs proposal discovery, review, deliberation, execution, accountability, dispute handling, and memory.

## The lifecycle

IsoniaOS frames governance as:

```text
Idea -> Proposal -> Review -> Decision -> Execution -> Accountability -> Memory
```

Each stage answers a different question:

- Idea: what problem or opportunity exists?
- Proposal: what action is being requested?
- Review: what risks, alternatives, and requirements apply?
- Decision: who approved, rejected, vetoed, or let the proposal expire?
- Execution: what happened after approval?
- Accountability: who owns follow-through and what proof exists?
- Memory: what should the organization remember later?

## Proposal to proof of execution

The early IsoniaOS wedge is:

```text
From proposal to proof of execution.
```

That means a passed decision should produce a visible record:

- what was approved;
- who or what had authority;
- who was responsible;
- what action was expected;
- what transaction or evidence proves follow-through;
- what remains blocked, failed, cancelled, or unresolved.

## Why this matters

Without lifecycle records, organizations lose context:

- similar proposals repeat old debates;
- grants are funded without durable milestone evidence;
- contributors and working groups lose accountability context;
- execution happens in private coordination channels;
- new participants depend on insiders to reconstruct history.

IsoniaOS makes the lifecycle inspectable without pretending every connected record has the same authority.
