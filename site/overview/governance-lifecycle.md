# Governance Lifecycle

IsoniaOS treats governance as a lifecycle:

```text
Idea -> Proposal -> Review -> Decision -> Execution -> Accountability -> Memory
```

Voting remains important, but it is not the whole process.

## Lifecycle stages

### Idea

An issue, need, risk, or opportunity is identified.

### Proposal

The idea becomes a structured proposal with scope, route, evidence requirements, and expected outcomes.

### Review

Relevant reviewers, bodies, delegates, or contributors evaluate the proposal and its risks.

### Decision

The organization approves, rejects, expires, cancels, or otherwise resolves the proposal through its governance process.

### Execution

Approved action is carried out through contracts, multisigs, external tools, contributors, or manual operations.

### Accountability

The organization tracks responsible parties, due dates, milestones, evidence, blockers, failures, and completion review.

### Memory

The outcome becomes part of institutional memory so future decisions can refer to context, precedent, proof, and lessons learned.

## Why this matters

A passed proposal is not the end of governance. It creates obligations. IsoniaOS is designed to keep those obligations visible and tied to evidence.
