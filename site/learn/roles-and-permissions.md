# Roles And Permissions

Roles explain responsibility. Permissions define what each role can do.

## Common Roles

| Role | Plain-language meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Admin | Configures the organization and core settings. |
| Reviewer | Checks a proposal before a decision. |
| Approver | Can approve a proposal under the configured route. |
| Executor | Performs or confirms an approved action. |
| Contributor | Creates proposals or completes assigned work. |
| Observer | Reads records without changing them. |

An organization can use different names, but the goal is the same: make responsibility visible.

## Common Permissions

Permissions answer questions such as:

- Who can create a proposal?
- Who can review it?
- Who can approve it?
- Who can block or veto it, if that route exists?
- Who can execute the approved action?
- Who can attach evidence?
- Who can update accountability status?
- Who can mark a record complete?

## Why This Matters

Hidden power weakens trust. If a participant sees a proposal status change, they should be able to understand why that person or group had permission to change it.

Good permission design also protects admins. It reduces confusion about who is expected to act and who is only reviewing, observing, or providing evidence.

## Example

Community Grants DAO might use:

- one admin group to configure the organization;
- a reviewer group to check applications;
- an approver group to approve or reject funding proposals;
- an executor group to complete the approved transaction or external action;
- contributors who submit work and attach evidence.
