# Accountability

Accountability is the record of follow-through after a decision.

It should help anyone answer:

- Who owns the next step?
- What is the current status?
- What evidence exists?
- What is late, blocked, failed, or complete?
- What still needs review?

## Accountability Record

An accountability record can include:

- linked proposal or decision;
- responsible person or group;
- due date or expected window;
- status;
- evidence;
- milestone notes;
- failure or cancellation reason;
- completion note;
- reviewer or confirming party;
- update history.

## Statuses

Useful statuses include:

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Not needed | The decision does not require follow-up. |
| Waiting | The decision passed but execution has not started. |
| In progress | Work has started but is not complete. |
| Blocked | Work cannot continue until a problem is resolved. |
| Executed | The primary action happened, but review may still be needed. |
| Complete | Execution and review are complete. |
| Failed | The obligation failed. |
| Cancelled | Follow-up was intentionally cancelled. |
| Unknown | The current status cannot be trusted or found. |

## Manual Updates

Manual updates are useful. They let an admin or reviewer record context that a contract or external record cannot show.

But manual updates should remain visible as manual updates. They do not automatically prove the approved intent was satisfied.

## Example

Community Grants DAO approves a grant. The accountability record assigns the contributor as responsible, sets a due date, and requires a deliverable link. If the due date passes without evidence, the record becomes overdue or blocked instead of disappearing.
