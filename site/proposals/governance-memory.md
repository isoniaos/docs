# Governance Memory

Governance memory is the durable record of what an organization decided, why it decided it, what happened after the decision, and what should be remembered later.

## What memory should preserve

IsoniaOS should preserve:

- proposals;
- reviews;
- discussions and summaries;
- vote or approval results;
- rationales;
- execution records;
- evidence links;
- disputes and resolutions;
- policy changes;
- delegate and contributor history;
- similar historical proposals;
- post-decision analysis.

## Why it matters

Without governance memory:

- new contributors cannot reconstruct decisions;
- similar proposals repeat old debates;
- risks and tradeoffs are forgotten;
- accountability depends on insiders;
- failed or delayed execution disappears.

## Public archive

The Public Governance Archive is the reader-facing memory layer.

It should help members, contributors, delegates, grantees, reviewers, and observers understand the decision lifecycle without needing private chat history or spreadsheets.
