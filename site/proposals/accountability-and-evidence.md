# Accountability and Evidence

Accountability records show what happened after a decision.

Core principle:

```text
A passed proposal creates an obligation, not just a result.
```

## Accountability fields

An accountability record may include:

- responsible party;
- due date;
- execution status;
- linked transaction hash;
- external proof;
- manual status updates;
- milestones;
- failure reason;
- cancellation reason;
- completion confirmation.

## Accountability statuses

Recommended follow-through statuses:

- not required;
- awaiting execution;
- in progress;
- blocked;
- executed;
- completed;
- failed;
- cancelled;
- unknown.

These statuses do not replace proposal lifecycle status.

## Evidence types

Evidence may include:

- onchain transaction;
- Safe transaction;
- block explorer link;
- GitHub pull request;
- published report;
- attestation;
- uploaded evidence;
- reviewer confirmation;
- external proof URL.

## Trust boundary

Manual evidence and external evidence should be labeled. They help participants evaluate follow-through, but they do not override contract state unless explicitly modeled and verified.
