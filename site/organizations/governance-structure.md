# Governance Structure

IsoniaOS models governance structure so participants can understand who has authority and which process applies.

## Core elements

An organization may define:

- governance bodies;
- roles and permissions;
- proposal types;
- policy routes;
- voting or decision modules;
- veto bodies;
- timelocks;
- executors;
- evidence requirements;
- accountability requirements.

## Policy routes

Policy routes connect proposal types to decision and execution requirements.

A route should explain:

- who can create a proposal;
- who reviews it;
- who approves it;
- whether a vote is required;
- whether veto is available;
- whether a timelock applies;
- who can execute;
- what evidence is expected after execution.

## Template posture

Templates can help organizations start from known patterns such as grants DAO, protocol operations DAO, treasury committee, or working group.

Templates are configuration blueprints, not authority by themselves. Authority-changing setup must be explicit and governed where applicable.
